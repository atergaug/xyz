Programs and Equipment I Use

Here are the programs and equipment I use for everything. I'm only putting here methods I consider tried and true and have used for a while.

Hardware I Use

External DVD/CD Drive
USB 2.0 interface. Multi-region support. Combination of CD-R/RW, DVD-R/RW, DVD+R/RW, DVD+R DL, DVD-R, DL and DVD-RAM drive functions into one drive.
SATA to USB Cable
USB 3.0 interface. Backup data from 2.5" and 3.5" drives via USB.
Microphone
I record most of my audio with a Razer Seiren Mini, people in comms say it sounds quality. Compared to other microphones under $100, I preferred the warm and bassy sound signature of the Seiren Mini. It does the job without having to postprocess the midrange or bass of voice recordings.

Software I Use

Priorities

I'm about getting things done reasonably quick and secure. I don't mind sacrificing convenience for privacy/security as long as it is reliable.

I like having MacOS-like bindings and have no preference running programs in the terminal or graphical interface. That said, I'm against the meme that things in the terminal are "cooler" or "nerdier". Terminals are good for most tasks, but useless for others, such as web browsing or looking at maps or images or modifying videos by NLE. Image/video editing can be done with imagemagick/ffmpeg, but only simple, repetitive tasks.

Basic

Operating System/Distribution
I use Pop!OS(site) which is a fork of Ubuntu primarily maintained by System76. Linux distributions are generally not distinct enough to have strong feelings about.
Terminal
I use terminator (site), which is one of the most extensible, yet easily customizable terminal emulators out there.
Shell
I use bash as a shell however, moving to zsh in the future as it has pretty much all of the features of bash with additional plugins for more advanced auto-completion and syntax coloring.
